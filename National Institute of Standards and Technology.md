The [National Institute of Science and Technology](https://www.nist.gov) is the official standards body of the US Federal Government.

Specifically applicable to IAM, they publish a number of standards related to Information Technology, and Cyber Security.

Notable publications include:
- [Special Publication 800-63: Digital Identity Guidelines](https://pages.nist.gov/800-63-4/sp800-63.html) 
- [Special Publication 800-53: Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/sp/800/53/r5/upd1/final) 
- [Federal Information Protection Standard 140-3: Security Requirements for Cryptographic Modules](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/fips/140-3/final)

NIST works closely with Canadian federal counterparts, and jointly runs the [Cryptographic Module Validation Program](https://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-module-validation-program) ([[CMVP]]) with the [[Canadian Centre for Cyber Security]]
