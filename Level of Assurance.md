Defined in Standard on Identity and Credential Assurance, published by the [[Treasury Board Secretariat]] (a Canadian Federal Government agency)
it is a measure used to denote the degree of confidence that for:
- Identity Assurance: an individual is who they claim to be; and, the electronic representation of a person is used exclusively by that person
- Credential Assurance: an individual has maintained control over a credential entrusted to them, and it has not been compromised

The numerical levels, and associated degrees are as follows:

| LoA | Degree of Confodence |
| :-: | :------------------: |
|  1  |        Little        |
|  2  |         Some         |
|  3  |         High         |
|  4  |      Very High       |

Criteria for Identity LoA is defined in the [Standard on Identity and Credential Assurance](https://www.tbs-sct.canada.ca/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=32612)

Criteria for Credential LoA is defined in [User authentication guidance for information technology systems (ITSP.30.031 v3)](https://www.cyber.gc.ca/en/guidance/user-authentication-guidance-information-technology-systems-itsp30031-v3) published by the [[Canadian Centre for Cyber Security]].

Level of Assurance is similar, but not exactly equivalent to [[Assurance Level]] defined by [[NIST]] and used by the US Federal Government.
