The function of validating that a subject (i.e. an entity asserting a specific identity) is legitimate, with a certain degree of confidence (i.e. [[Level of Assurance]] / [[Assurance Level]])

The process by which a subject is authenticated is roughly:
1. A subject presents themselves and provides credentials which include:
	 - an [[Identifier]] which uniquely identifies them; and,
	 - an [[Authenticator]] to "prove" they are legitimately the entity they claim to be
2. The authenticator is validated to ensure it corresponds to the identity the subject is asserting (based on the identifier they supplied), and that the authenticator has not been revoked (or, if applicable, is expired)
3. Additional authenticators may be requested, presented, and validated (i.e. [[Multi-factor Authentication]] is performed) to achieve the desired degree of confidence.
