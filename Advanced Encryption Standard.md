Abbreviated as [[AES]]

A symmetric key encryption scheme standardized by [[NIST]] in their [FIPS 197: Advanced Encryption Standard](https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/fips/197/final) publication.

AES has become the industry standards for bulk [[Encryption]] of data, both [[At Rest]] and [[In-transit]].  Many computing devices implement AES in hardware to speed the encryption and decryption of data.

AES is considered [[Quantum resistant]] when keys of sufficient size are used - i.e. 256-bits (preferred) or 128-bits.  However, exchange of symmetric keys used with AES may be susceptible to attacks using [[Quantum computing]], unless [[Quantum resistant]] key encapsulation algorithms are used.
